Whack-A-Mole!

This is the final project for my Digital Systems course, in which we learned how to develop complex digital systems and analyse their efficiency. The game utilizes hardware interrupts and Arduino timers, and works by the user first turning the device on by pressing a button, choosing a difficulty, and managing to hit the buttons corresponding to the lit light as fast as possible. The score is displayed on the computer screen at the end.

Multipurpose Alarm Clock Redesign

I worked on this project for my Human Factors group with a team of other highly talented individuals. We were faced with the task of redesigning an old alarm clock that one of us had using human factors design principles. I learned and was able to apply many techniques such as developing a situation of concern, conducting Fault Tree analysis, Hierarchical task analysis, conduct Heuristic evaluations and assess the usability of the display and controls. I then generated this Solidworks model of our proposed design.

cHello

The cHello is an electronic Cello that incorporates capacitive and resistive touch to produce sound. Output is in MIDI, and signal mapping is done via Teensy. From this project I was able to obtain experience in additive manufacturing, laser cutting, and hardware development.
